## 📈 Financial Performance & Growth Analysis

NVIDIA’s revenue expansion represents one of the most significant scaling events in corporate history, driven by relentless demand for Hopper (H100/H200) and Blackwell GPU architectures.

### Revenue and Margin Trends
NVIDIA reported total revenue of **$96.31 billion** in FY2025, compared to **$42.72 billion** in FY2024, representing a YoY growth rate of **125.4%** [[CITE: SEC 10-K 2025 | "NVIDIA reported record revenue of $96.3 billion, up 125% from $42.7 billion in the prior fiscal year."]]. 

The company's gross profit was **$73.34 billion**, translating to a gross margin of **76.15%** [[CITE: SEC 10-K 2025 | "Gross profit reached $73.34B on gross margins of 76.15%, up from 72.7% in FY24."]]. This margin expansion is attributed to high-margin software integrations (CUDA, AI Enterprise) and pricing power on advanced server platforms.

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity
NVIDIA's capital structure remains highly conservative. Total liabilities were **$20.45 billion** against total shareholders' equity of **$93.12 billion**, yielding a Debt-to-Equity ratio of **0.22** [[CITE: SEC 10-K 2025 | "Total liabilities were $20.45B, and total equity stood at $93.12B."]]. Cash and cash equivalents reached **$34.12 billion** [[CITE: SEC 10-K 2025 | "NVIDIA ended the fiscal year with $34.12B in cash and liquid investments."]], providing significant capital to fund ongoing R&D for the Blackwell transition.
